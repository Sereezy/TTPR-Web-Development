The Best Steak Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 thick-cut steak (Ribeye or New York Strip)
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • High smoke point oil (vegetable, canola, or avocado oil)
  • Butter
  • Fresh Hrebs like rosemary or thyme (optional)
  • Garlic cloves, smashed (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preparation
  2. Allow the steak to come to room temperature for 30-45 minutes. This helps ensure even cooking.
  3. Pat the steak dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture. This is crucial for achieving a good sear.
  4. Season both sides of the steak generously with salt and pepper.

Pan Searing

  1. Heat a cast iron skillet over high heat until it's very hot and shimmering.
  2. Add a few tablespoons of high smoke point oil to the hot skillet.
  3. Carefully place the steak in the hot pan. You should hear it sizzle immediately.
  4. Sear the first side without moving it for about 3-4 minutes, or until a deep brown crust develops.
  5. Flip the steak and sear the other side for the same amount of time.
  6. For thicker cuts, use tongs to sear the edges by holding the steak upright on its sides.

    7. Finishing

  7. Reduce the heat to medium-low and add the butter to the skillet.
  8. Add fresh herbs and smashed garlic cloves to the pan.
  9. Once the butter is melted, tilt the skillet and use a spoon to baste the steak with the melted butter, herbs, and garlic.
  10. Continue basting until the steak reaches your desired internal temperature. Use a meat thermometer to ensure accuracy. Note that the internal temperature will continue to rise a few degrees after removing the steak from the heat.
    • Medium-rare: 120-125°F (130°F when served)
    • Medium: 130-135°F (140°F when served)
    • Medium-well: 140-145°F (150°F when served)
    • Well done: 150-155°F (160°F when served)

    Resting

  11. Transfer the cooked steak to a plate or cutting board.
  12. Tent loosely with aluminum foil and let the steak rest for 5-10 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in a more tender and juicy steak.

    13. Serving

  13. Slice the steak against the grain (perpendicular to the lines in the meat).
  14. Serve immediately, perhaps with the leftover butter and herbs from the pan.
